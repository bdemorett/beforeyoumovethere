Moving to Edina, MN: What to Know Before You Go

Edina is one of the most recognizable suburbs in the Minneapolis area. It is known for strong schools, polished shopping districts, established neighborhoods, parks, medical access, and a convenient location just southwest of Minneapolis. It is also expensive, reputation-heavy, and not the right fit for every household that says it wants a “good suburb.”

Quick verdict

Edina is a premium, practical suburb for people who can afford the tradeoff.

Edina works best for households that want a mature inner-ring suburb with strong schools, convenient Minneapolis access, established neighborhoods, good parks, and a polished daily-life setup. It works less well if your top priorities are affordability, newer suburban growth, a looser feel, or getting the most house possible for the money.

Cost and housing

Edina is not where you go for a bargain

Edina’s biggest practical issue is cost. The suburb has a strong reputation, desirable school access, convenient geography, and limited supply in many established areas. That combination keeps housing pressure high. A buyer moving from a more affordable metro may be surprised by how quickly ordinary family homes become expensive here.

The value case for Edina is not cheap housing. It is the package: school reputation, Minneapolis access, parks, daily convenience, medical proximity, and a mature suburb that already has a clear identity. If that package matters to you, Edina can make sense. If you mainly want value per square foot, other Twin Cities suburbs may fit better.

Family fit

Edina is one of the area’s classic family-suburb choices

Edina is often on the shortlist for families because it offers a more established version of suburban life: schools with a strong reputation, parks and recreation, youth activities, libraries, shopping, and a location that keeps Minneapolis accessible. It feels built for long-term routines rather than short-term experimentation.

The tradeoff is that a strong family-suburb reputation attracts demand. That can mean higher prices, competitive buying conditions, and a social environment that may feel more status-aware than some movers prefer.

Area feel

Different parts of Edina can feel more urban, suburban, or residential

Edina is not just one uniform suburb. The right fit depends on whether you want walkable shopping access, a quiet residential pocket, easier highway access, or a more traditional family-neighborhood setup.

Commute and access

Location is one of Edina’s biggest advantages

Edina sits close enough to Minneapolis that it can work for people who want suburb life without fully disconnecting from the city. Access to major roads also makes the suburb practical for many regional routines, including office commutes, airport trips, medical appointments, and shopping.

The catch is that convenience depends on your exact address and commute direction. A home that looks perfect on paper may feel different if school traffic, winter driving, or highway access adds friction to your routine.

Daily life

Edina feels polished, convenient, and highly managed

Edina’s daily-life appeal is not just one big feature. It is the accumulation of many smaller conveniences: retail districts, parks, medical access, organized recreation, strong municipal identity, and quick access to Minneapolis. For many households, that makes the suburb feel easy to live in.

For others, that same polish can feel expensive, competitive, or a little too curated. Edina is a great fit for some movers precisely because it is established and high-expectation. It is a weaker fit if you want a looser or more value-oriented suburb.

Biggest strengths

  • Strong school reputation and family-suburb appeal
  • Close-in location near Minneapolis
  • Established neighborhoods with mature trees and local identity
  • Good parks, shopping, medical access, and daily convenience
  • Multiple lifestyle pockets, from 50th & France to quieter residential areas

Biggest downsides

  • High housing costs compared with many Twin Cities suburbs
  • Competitive buyer demand in desirable areas
  • Not the best value-per-square-foot option
  • May feel too polished or status-conscious for some movers
  • Older homes may require updates despite premium prices
Mistakes

Biggest mistakes people make before moving to Edina

  1. Assuming reputation automatically means fit. Edina is strong, but not every household needs what it offers.
  2. Underestimating housing cost. Premium suburbs can make ordinary homes feel expensive.
  3. Ignoring exact location. Commute, school boundaries, parks, and shopping access can vary by address.
  4. Comparing Edina only to Minneapolis. The real decision may include St. Louis Park, Bloomington, Richfield, Minnetonka, or Eden Prairie.
  5. Overlooking older-home maintenance. Established neighborhoods can come with renovation and upkeep costs.
FAQ

Edina FAQ

Is Edina a good place to live?

Yes, for people who want a premium, established suburb with strong schools, parks, shopping, and close access to Minneapolis.

Is Edina expensive?

Yes. Edina is generally one of the more expensive and in-demand suburbs in the Minneapolis area.

Is Edina better than Minneapolis?

It depends. Edina is better for many households that want suburban routine, schools, and lower-friction daily life. Minneapolis is better for people who want city neighborhoods, denser amenities, and a more urban lifestyle.

Who should avoid Edina?

Movers who prioritize affordability, newer outer-suburb homes, or maximum house for the money may find better fits elsewhere in the metro.
